skills/managing-research-plans/references/plan-rubric.md:9:The rubric scores **five channels, each 0–3**. Each channel is one place where authorship and control can leak from the human to the agent. There is **no pass/fail threshold** — a plan is scored, not gated. The score is never the deliverable: the rubric produces a **diagnosis** (a five-number profile, the biggest leak, the open forks to fix) that points at the next revision.

skills/managing-research-plans/references/plan-rubric.md:17:## The five channels (0–3 each)
skills/managing-research-plans/references/plan-rubric.md:56:Sum the five channels for a 0–15 total, but **read the profile, not the total**. A missing channel must not hide behind a strong total: `3·3·3·0·3 = 12/15` is not "80% good," it is a plan with no validation. So the five-number profile is the headline, and **any channel scoring 0 is flagged as a missing control channel**. There is no band or headline percentage that lets one channel's strength cover another's absence.
